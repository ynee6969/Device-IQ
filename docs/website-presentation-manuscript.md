# DeviceIQ Website Presentation Manuscript

## 5-Minute Oral Script

### 0:00-0:25 Greeting
"Good day, everyone. Our website is called DeviceIQ, and it is a phone discovery and comparison platform built to help users choose the right phone faster."

### 0:25-1:05 Introduction
"We chose this project because buying a phone can be confusing. People usually jump between many tabs just to compare price, camera, battery, performance, and design. That process takes time, and it is easy to miss important details."

### 1:05-1:45 Website Rationale
"The main rationale of DeviceIQ is to organize that decision-making process in one place. Instead of making the user browse several websites, we bring the catalog, comparison tool, favorites, and detail pages into one clean flow."

"The website is designed for both casual visitors and more serious buyers. Casual visitors can browse immediately, while returning users can save favorites and continue their comparison later."

### 1:45-2:30 Problem Being Solved
"The problem we are solving is information overload. A user may know the phone brand they want, but still needs to compare models, check the spec summary, and look at the price. DeviceIQ reduces that friction by turning a scattered search into a guided experience."

### 2:30-3:10 Purpose Of The Project
"The purpose of the project is to make phone selection simpler, more focused, and more user-friendly. The site is not just a catalog. It is a decision-support website that helps users narrow choices and feel more confident before they buy."

### 3:10-4:05 Frontend Design And UI/UX
"On the frontend, we used a clean card-based layout, a dark glassmorphism style, and strong accent colors so the interface feels modern and easy to scan. The homepage introduces the product story, the dashboard focuses on browsing, and the compare page highlights differences side by side."

"We also kept the navigation consistent across pages with a sticky header on desktop and a mobile tab bar on smaller screens. That makes the experience easier to understand on both phone and laptop."

### 4:05-4:35 Responsiveness
"Responsiveness is a major part of the design. The layout rearranges itself for mobile screens, filters become easier to reach, and the compare and card sections stack vertically when there is less space. This way, the site stays usable without losing clarity."

### 4:35-5:00 Conclusion
"In summary, DeviceIQ was built to simplify phone shopping by putting browsing, comparison, and saving in one organized interface. Our goal was to make the website practical, readable, and helpful for real users."

"Thank you, and we are ready for your questions."

## Speaker Cues

- Start calm and confident.
- Emphasize "one place", "compare", and "faster" because those are the core ideas.
- Pause briefly after the problem statement so the audience can absorb it.
- Smile slightly when you mention the user benefits.

## Timing Guide

- Greeting: 20 to 25 seconds
- Introduction: 35 to 40 seconds
- Rationale: 35 to 40 seconds
- Problem: 35 to 40 seconds
- Purpose: 30 to 35 seconds
- UI/UX: 45 to 55 seconds
- Responsiveness: 25 to 30 seconds
- Conclusion: 20 to 25 seconds

## Transition Lines

- "With that in mind, the main reason we built the site is..."
- "From there, the website is organized into..."
- "On the design side, we focused on..."
- "To make it work on smaller screens..."
- "So overall, the project gives users..."

## Emphasis Suggestions

- Emphasize "search, compare, and save".
- Emphasize "information overload" when describing the problem.
- Emphasize "optional sign-in" when discussing favorites.
- Emphasize "responsive" and "easy to scan" when describing the UI.

## Pause Suggestions

- Pause after the opening sentence.
- Pause after explaining the problem.
- Pause before the conclusion.

## Backup Lines For Nervous Presenters

- "Our website helps users compare phones more easily."
- "The goal is to reduce confusion during phone shopping."
- "The design keeps the experience clean and simple."
- "The layout adjusts for mobile and desktop screens."

## Suggested Body Language

- Stand straight and keep both feet planted.
- Use one hand to gesture when explaining the problem and solution.
- Point to screenshots instead of reading every word.
- Make eye contact at the end of each main section.
